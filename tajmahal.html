Welcome To Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal is a mausoleum complex in Agra, western Uttar Pradesh state, northern India. It is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture (a blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles). The Taj Mahal is also one of the world’s most iconic monuments, visited by millions of tourists each year. The complex was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983.
The Taj Mahal was built as a tomb for Mumtaz Mahal (“Chosen One of the Palace”) by her husband, the Mughal emperor Shah Jahān (reigned 1628–58). She died in childbirth in 1631, after having been the emperor’s inseparable companion since their marriage in 1612.

Key Facts about the Taj Mahal

  1. construction period
    • the construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.
    • it took approximately 21 years to complete the entire complex.
  2. Architectural Features
    • The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens.
    • The main mausoleum is topped with a large dome and surrounded by four minarets.
  3. Cultural Significance
    • The Taj Mahal is a symbol of love and is often referred to as a "monument of love" due to its origin as a mausoleum for Mumtaz Mahal.
    • It attracts millions of visitors from around the world each year, making it one of the most popular tourist destinations in India.
